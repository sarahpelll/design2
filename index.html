I. Course Goals


01. Consider technology’s role in acts of making

Throughout the projects showcased above, the utilization of technology has been central to understanding how I can achieve more in design, no pun intended. Although perhaps a joke at the time, Dave Chapelle was right when he said "modern problems require modern solutions," at least in the design industry. Growing my own understanding of the Adobe suite and ways I can use digital tools to create things is an incomparable skill.

02. Leverage Design Principles.

Learning design principles is an ongoing process throughout both this class and throughout my career. Different professions and different audiences demand different design principles, and this is truly a gateway to learning fundamentals that can be applied universally. One thing that I learned that I could immediately use comes from the UX research I conducted. I learned that some of my designs tend to lack personality and are difficult to relate back to my person. This makes me think more about how different online profiles I have reflect me. Overall, every aspect of design needs to be incredibly intentional.

03.Recognize and respond to ethical concerns.

One of the largest ethical issues I have held with these projects was the usage of unaccessible software to develop designs. It is very important to understand that all of these design processes could be completed for free without the usage of Adobe, Canva, and other softwares that are extremely costly. I believe that the world of design gets closer and closer to an area of elitism that is extremely disturbing because of the costliness of the preferred softwares.

4. Engage with others in a workshop environment

Learning with my peers, aiding them and asking for help in return, has been an integral part of successfully completing this first portion of my portfolio. I will continue to ask for feedback and help and also offering help to anyone who needs it. I do have to admit though, I was out sick with COVID for one workshop, so I missed out on that help.

5. Practice revision within and across media forms

I believe that the largest example of revision in this project would simply be comparing my preliminary logos to my final logos. Although this is most obvious, as I changed several icons and added text after receiving feedback, I also edited my other projects as I was creating them. For example, my first draft of my "Cartoon Yourself" project actually is a completely different picture. I switched to a picture that was more clear so that I could better complete the project. I also used many different methods that I learned from many sources when completing that project. I think that design is truly a blend of everything the designer has ever seen in their life.

6. Learn to present your work

Presenting has never been an area I truly shy from in school settings; it is simply a necessary evil. In this class, the presentation settings have been quite intimate, at most presenting to roughly 20 of my peers. I am very glad to recognize that this is an incredibly respectful crowd that I have had to present in front. The more daunting presentation for me was within the UX Research project. It taught me that being confident in a conversation is key to it going well. Going into every interview, I was apprehensive that the participant perhaps did not want to be there or what-not. In reality, people are happy to share respect and are more likely to do so if you are more confident and clear.

I. Course Goals


01. Consider technology’s role in acts of making

Technology is an ongoing part of this class. This unit, I grew my understanding of InDesign greatly, especially when doing multiple-page spreads. Also, we learned to manage projects across multiple platforms. In our Design Consultation, we conducted interviews via Zoom, communicated with our clients via e-mail, collected data using Google Forms, and I made my final designs using Canva. All-in-all, learning the ways these different programs add to my design-making process is important.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

These projects I focused on typography. At times, I can become perfectionistic about fonts, but this unit, I decided to formulate it. This allowed me to make my projects more coherent. Additionally, this unit focused on porportions, especially when working on the Design Consultation. It was difficult to fit so much information into such a small amount of space.

3.Recognize and respond to ethical concerns.

In this unit, we began to focus on understanding accessibility issues. Not only do we need to focus on obvious disabilities, but we should also be weary of people who struggle in silence. It is important to always design with intent so that it is widely accessible.

4. Engage with others in a workshop environment

In this past unit, the class has developed to be more unitary. The group asks each other questions and is overwhelmingly constructive when giving feedback.

5. Practice revision within and across media forms

One of the largest examples of revision in this unit was when we used an external source's feedback to catalyze our revision ideas. In the Design Consultation project, the client feedback shaped the final draft for the project.

6. Learn to present your work

The most important way we learned to present our work on the Short Form Portfolio was when we had to deliver designs to a client. It was extremely nerve-racking to have someone critique my work, but it ended up being completely constructive.

Course Goals


1. Consider technology’s role in acts of making

This class has encapsulated many different ways that technology can be used within our careers and our lives. Ranging from Adobe skills to coding, we have grown our knowledge of technology infinitely.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

This class taught us many design skills. These are especially prevalent within the brand design project, as we were allowed creative freedom and forced to make our own decisions. The hardest design principle that I found was font usage. I think it is extremely difficult to find a healthy balance of serif, mono, script, and so on.

3.Recognize and respond to ethical concerns.

This unit we spent a great deal of time discussing accessibility within our design. One way I attempted to incorporate this through my work was by ensuring contrast was strong and fonts were clear and readable. Although my acknowledgement of accessiblity is not perfect, I have gained more skills and a larger understanding of why my designs may be inaccessible for varying groups of people.

4. Engage with others in a workshop environment

Throughout this semester we have largely done work outside of class and then brought our projects into workshops to receive feedback. This feedback is key to the design process in that it gives us insight into how our designs will be perceived in a low-stakes environment.

5. Practice revision within and across media forms

This unit, I did the most revision on my ten-page spread and my brand design. For the ten-page spread, I added more color and better organization into my project after receiving feedback. For my brand design project, I used user experience feedback to edit my designs and make design choices.

6. Learn to present your work

This unit we presented in a few forms, firstly and most commonly was within small groups, as we did this with each of our projects. Secondly, we had the opportunity to present and receive feedback from the entire class, I did this for my ten-page spread and received a lot of feedback that allowed me to make the changes aforementioned. Lastly, we had to present to people outside of the class for our brand design project.
